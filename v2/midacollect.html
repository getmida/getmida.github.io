MidaCollect

MidaCollect is a digital collections platform designed to drive repayment through structured, data-driven workflows.

Talk to Us

Trusted by leading institutions in Africa

The Problem It Solves

Collections often fail not because borrowers refuse to pay, but because lenders rely on rigid instruments, poorly timed reminders, and disconnected engagement channels. Generic repayment tools lack context, ignore borrower behavior, and escalate too late — leading to missed repayments, damaged trust, and lower recovery rates.

MidaCollect replaces ad-hoc collections with structured, intelligent execution. It layers advanced logic on top of standard repayment instruments, coordinates borrower engagement across channels, and enables lenders to act early, turning repayment from a reactive process into a controlled, ethical system.

Key Capabilities

Repayment Instrument Setup & Tokenization

Configure and manage repayment instruments such as direct debits and due-date collections with advanced logic layered on top.

Digital Collections Workflows

Design structured collections journeys that adapt dynamically to borrower behavior and repayment status.

Smart, Continuous Collections

Automate repayment attempts and engagement sequences using real-time signals and borrower profiles.

Localized & Ethical Collections Logic

Apply region-specific rules, messaging, and engagement patterns while maintaining ethical standards.

Collections Analytics

Track repayment performance, channel effectiveness, and recovery outcomes with real-time analytics.

Ethics By Design

MidaCollect embeds ethical collections principles into every workflow. Engagement strategies are designed to respect borrowers, prioritize transparency, and protect lender reputation, while still driving strong repayment outcomes.

Ethics are not an afterthought or a policy layer. They are built directly into how collections logic operates, when customers are engaged, and how escalation occurs.

  • Respectful borrower engagement at appropriate times
  • Transparent communication and clear next steps
  • Graduated escalation based on borrower circumstances
